You already know *what* money is. This course is the *story of how it got there* — and it's wilder than any textbook lets on. Money isn't a fixed thing handed down from on high; it's a 5,000-year-old invention humanity keeps **re-engineering** under the pressure of war, trade imbalances, runaway inflation, and collapsing trust.

We zoom in on the **20th century**, when money changed shape more violently than in all the millennia before it. Across five lessons you'll trace:

- **The gold standard** — when a pound or a dollar was a fixed weight of metal, and how that golden chain both disciplined nations and dragged them into depression when it snapped.
- **Weimar hyperinflation** — World War I prints money off the gold leash, and 1923 Germany shows the world what a dying currency looks like: wheelbarrows of cash for a loaf of bread.
- **Bretton Woods (1944)** — the most ambitious monetary deal ever struck, where 44 nations pegged their currencies to the dollar and the dollar to gold, and invented the IMF and World Bank to police it.
- **The 1971 Nixon shock** — the Sunday night a TV broadcast quietly ended gold convertibility, launching the **pure fiat era** and floating exchange rates we still live in today.
- **Electronic money** — the eurodollar market, wire transfers, credit cards, and ATMs that turned cash into ledger entries.
- **Bitcoin's origins** — after the **2008 financial crisis** shattered trust in banks, the 2009 genesis block makes money-as-**code** real.

For each turn you'll learn *why* the transformation happened and *what* was traded away — the foundation the whole finance ladder is built on. A capstone final exam proves the timeline stuck.
