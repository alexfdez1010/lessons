Neural networks are the engine behind modern AI — from image recognition to
language models. But under the hood they are built from a surprisingly simple
idea: a tiny unit that takes some numbers, weighs them, and decides how strongly
to fire.

This topic starts from that single unit and works up to the **activation
functions** that let networks model the messy, non-linear world. No heavy math
required — just curiosity.
