You already know what a token is and why a dollar holds its value. Stablecoins sit exactly where those two ideas meet: a **crypto token engineered to be worth one dollar**, programmable and borderless, but as steady as the cash in your pocket. It sounds boring. It is, in fact, the plumbing of nearly all of crypto — most trading, lending and payments run on it — and at roughly **\$320 billion** in circulation, it is far too big to ignore.

This topic opens every kind of stablecoin all the way up. We start with **what a peg even is** and the quiet arbitrage that keeps a coin pinned to \$1. Then we work through the three designs that matter: **fiat-backed** coins like USDT and USDC, where the whole question is *what's really in the reserve*; **crypto-backed** dollars like DAI, minted as over-collateralized loans against locked crypto and policed by liquidations; and **algorithmic** coins, which try to hold the peg with code and faith alone — and whose **death spiral** vaporized Terra's UST and \$40 billion with it. We finish with the hard part: how stablecoins **depeg**, why USDC briefly fell to \$0.88 the weekend Silicon Valley Bank failed, a risk map you can actually use, and the new rulebooks — the **GENIUS Act** and **MiCA** — now drawing the lines. By the end, "it's just a digital dollar" stops being a hand-wave and becomes a machine you can take apart.
