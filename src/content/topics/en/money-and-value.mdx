Before compound interest, before stocks, before a single Sharpe ratio — there's the
deceptively simple question almost no one stops to ask: *what is money, actually?*
This is the root of the whole finance ladder, and it assumes you know **absolutely
nothing** yet. No jargon you haven't been handed. No balance sheet you're expected
to have seen. Just the bedrock vocabulary — money, payment, saving, borrowing,
interest, account, balance, and **ledger** — that every later course quietly leans on.

We'll start where money itself started: with **barter**, and the maddening problem
of needing to find someone who wants your bread *and* happens to be selling the shoes
you want, at the same time. Money is the fix. From there we'll watch a payment actually
happen — who pays whom, what flows back, and why "Approved" on a card reader doesn't
yet mean the money has moved. Then we'll follow money through your own life: in as
**income**, out as **expense**, and what the leftover (or the shortfall) means for
**saving** versus **borrowing**.

The course ends inside the most underrated invention in finance: the **ledger** — the
humble running list of who has what. Your bank balance is really just a line on one
of these. Get comfortable here and the rest of finance stops being intimidating and
starts being *legible*. Four short lessons, then a capstone exam to prove it stuck.
