You already know what a call and a put are worth, how the Greeks move, and how volatility itself trades. Now meet what professionals actually *build* with those parts. **Exotic options** are derivatives whose payoff depends on more than just the final price — on whether a level was *touched*, on the *average* or the *extreme* of the path, on an all-or-nothing trigger. **Structured products** glue those exotics onto a bond and sell the bundle to investors as a single "note" with a friendly name and a tempting coupon.

This is the **expert** capstone of the derivatives track. The goal is not to admire the financial engineering — it's to **see through it**. By the end you'll be able to take any structured product apart into the bond and options it's really made of, price each block, spot the option you're secretly *short*, and total up the costs the brochure forgot to mention.

- **Digital (binary) options** — all-or-nothing payoffs, why a digital is the limit of a tight **call spread**, and the violent **discontinuous delta** that makes them a hedging headache near the strike.
- **Barrier options** — knock-ins and knock-outs, up and down, the elegant **in–out parity** that ties them back to the vanilla, and the **reverse knock-out** whose delta explodes right at the barrier.
- **Path-dependent options** — **Asian** options that settle on the *average* (cheaper, harder to manipulate) and **lookbacks** that pay off the best price you never had to time.
- **Autocallables & cliquets** — the best-selling retail structure decoded observation by observation: autocall barrier, coupon barrier, **memory** coupons, and the deep **knock-in put** you're really short.
- **Structured notes, decomposed** — the master skill: every note = a **zero-coupon bond + an option strip**. Principal-protected notes, participation rates, and the **reverse convertible** that's just a bond minus a put.
- **Payoff engineering & hidden costs** — how desks assemble bespoke payoffs, where the **markup, bid–ask, dividend and funding assumptions** hide, the **issuer credit risk** you take on, and how to judge whether a product is worth it.

By the end you'll decompose any structured product into its building blocks, price digitals, barriers, Asians and lookbacks well enough to sanity-check a quote, read an autocallable's term sheet without flinching, and — above all — answer the only question that matters about a structured note: *what am I really long, what am I really short, and who is getting paid?*
