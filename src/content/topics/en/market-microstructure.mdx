Stock Markets & Funds taught you the order types — market, limit, stop. This course
zooms all the way in to where those orders actually meet: the **limit order book**, the
microscopic machine that turns a stream of buy and sell instructions into a price.

This is the layer most investors never see and most courses skip. It's also where the
real money is made and lost in fractions of a cent, multiplied by billions of shares.
You'll learn:

- **The order book** — the live, two-sided ledger of resting orders, and how a match
  actually happens (price–time priority, the queue you stand in).
- **The bid–ask spread** — why every asset has two prices, and the three distinct risks
  a market maker bundles into that gap.
- **Makers vs takers** — who supplies liquidity, who consumes it, and why exchanges pay
  one and charge the other.
- **Liquidity, depth & slippage** — what "deep" really means, how to read the book, and
  exactly how a big order walks the levels and pays for it.
- **Tick sizes** — why the smallest possible price increment quietly shapes spreads,
  queues, and whole trading strategies.
- **Market makers & inventory risk** — how someone quoting both sides all day avoids
  getting buried under a position that moves against them.
- **Adverse selection** — the maker's nightmare: trading against someone who knows
  something you don't, and why it's the deepest reason spreads exist at all.
- **Fragmentation & dark pools** — why "the" market is really dozens of venues, and what
  trades in the dark.

Finish this and the plumbing behind `mev-and-ordering` and the central limit order books
of `polymarket-prediction-markets` will read like a language you already speak.
