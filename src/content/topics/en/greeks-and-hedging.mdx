Pricing a single option is a one-time calculation. **Running a book of them is a job** — a continuous, twitchy, profit-and-loss-bleeding job, and it is run almost entirely through the Greeks. Options Pricing taught you where delta, gamma, theta, vega and rho come from (they are just the derivatives of Black–Scholes). This course is about *living off them*: what a trader actually does, hour by hour, to keep a position alive and pull money out of it.

We start by getting close to the two Greeks that dominate a hedged book: **delta** — your exposure to the stock, the dial you keep pinned to zero — and **gamma** — how fast delta itself moves, the reason "pinned to zero" never stays pinned. Then the **clock Greeks**: **theta**, the rent the market charges you for holding optionality, and **rho**, the quiet sensitivity to interest rates. We'll see the central bargain of every options position laid bare — **gamma and theta are two sides of one coin**: you can't have the convexity without paying the decay, and you can't earn the decay without being short the convexity.

From there, **vega and the volatility surface**: implied volatility isn't one number, it's a whole landscape that bends across strike (the **skew** and **smile**) and across maturity (the **term structure**), and a real book is long or short *vega* at every point on it. Then the craft itself: **delta-neutral hedging** — why you re-hedge, how often, and the brutal tradeoff that more frequent rebalancing shrinks your hedging error but runs up transaction costs until there's an optimum. We turn that into the trade that makes long-gamma desks money: **gamma scalping**, where the hedge forces you to buy low and sell high, and your P&L comes down to one comparison — **did realized volatility beat the implied vol you paid?** Finally, **P&L attribution**: at day's end, break the change in your book's value into a delta piece, a gamma piece, a theta piece, a vega piece and a rho piece, until every dollar is explained — the discipline that separates a trader who got lucky from one who knows *why*.

This is the most hands-on quant course on the ladder: every Greek earns its keep with an analogy, a worked number, and a chart you can poke. Bring Options Pricing — you'll use all of it.
