You already know what a blockchain *is* — a shared, append-only ledger that a crowd of
strangers keeps honest without a boss. **Bitcoin** is the first and most famous machine
built on that idea, and this topic opens it all the way up. No hand-waving, no "it's
complicated," no assuming you've ever owned a satoshi.

We build it as a ladder. First **what Bitcoin is and why it exists** — the problem it was
designed to solve. Then the **ledger and mining** that keep it running, and the
**proof-of-work** race that decides who writes the next page. We'll see exactly why there
will only ever be **21 million coins** and what a **halving** does to the flow of new
supply. Then the part that's actually *yours*: **keys, addresses and wallets** — and why
"not your keys, not your coins" is the whole game. We'll follow a **transaction** from
your screen to a confirmed block and figure out where the **fee** goes, learn why running
a **full node** is the ultimate "don't trust, verify," and finish with the **Lightning
Network** — Bitcoin's answer to buying a coffee without waiting ten minutes. Get these
eight and Bitcoin stops being a vibe and starts being mechanics.
