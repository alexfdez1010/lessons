You already know what a blockchain *is* — a shared, append-only ledger a crowd of strangers
keeps honest without a boss. **Bitcoin** is the first and most famous machine built on that
idea, and this topic opens it all the way up — no hand-waving, no assuming you've ever owned
a satoshi.

We build it as a ladder, eight rungs at a time:

- **What Bitcoin is and why it exists** — the problem it was designed to solve.
- **The ledger and mining** — the machinery that keeps it running.
- **Proof-of-work** — the race that decides who writes the next page.
- **The 21-million cap and halvings** — why supply is fixed and what cuts the flow of new coins.
- **Keys, addresses and wallets** — the part that's actually *yours*, and why "not your keys, not your coins" is the whole game.
- **A transaction and its fee** — following one from your screen to a confirmed block, and where the fee goes.
- **Full nodes** — the ultimate "don't trust, verify."
- **The Lightning Network** — Bitcoin's answer to buying a coffee without waiting ten minutes.

Get these eight and Bitcoin stops being a vibe and starts being mechanics.
