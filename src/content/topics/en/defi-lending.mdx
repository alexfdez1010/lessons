You already know how Ethereum runs code nobody can switch off, and you know what interest, APR and APY mean. Point those two ideas at the oldest business in finance — lending money at one rate and borrowing it at another — and you get a **money market with no bank in the middle**: a set of smart contracts where idle assets earn yield and locked collateral unlocks cash, all without a loan officer, an application form, or anyone who knows your name.

This topic opens that machine all the way up. We start with **what DeFi lending even is** — pooled liquidity instead of a banker, algorithmic rates, and the surprise that every loan is *over*-collateralized. Then we follow the borrower's-eye view: **collateral, loan-to-value and borrowing power**; the **health factor** that measures how close you are to trouble; and **liquidation**, the automated repo-man that keeps lenders whole. We dissect the **interest-rate curve** that turns utilization into a price, meet the **receipt tokens** (aTokens, cTokens) that quietly compound in your wallet, and confront the things that actually break — **oracle failures, bad debt, and protocol risk**. By the end, Aave, Compound and MakerDAO/Sky stop being logos and start being mechanisms you can reason about — and, if you choose, use without getting liquidated.
