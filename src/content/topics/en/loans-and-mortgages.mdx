You already know what interest is, and you know the difference between APR and APY. Now point that machinery at the most common financial decision a person ever makes: **borrowing money**. A car loan, a student loan, a credit card, a mortgage — they're all the same engine wearing different clothes, and that engine is **compound interest running in reverse**, working for the lender and against you.

This topic teaches borrowing from the **customer's side of the desk**. We start with the anatomy of a loan — **principal, interest, the rate, the term** — and what makes a mortgage special. Then we crack open the **amortization schedule**: why your early payments are almost all interest, how the split flips over time, and exactly where your money goes each month. We compare **fixed vs variable rates** and untangle **APR vs APY** as lenders actually quote them, so you can read the **total cost of credit** instead of just the monthly payment. We cover the **down payment, loan-to-value, and the mortgage insurance** it triggers, plus the **fees and closing costs** that hide in the fine print. And we finish with the things that change the deal after you sign: **early repayment, refinancing, default, and foreclosure**. By the end, no loan document can bluff you.
