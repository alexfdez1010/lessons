A car loan, a student loan, a credit card, a mortgage — they're all the same engine wearing different clothes, and that engine is **compound interest running in reverse**, working for the lender and against you. This topic teaches borrowing from the **customer's side of the desk** so no loan document can ever bluff you again.

Here's what you'll walk away knowing:

- **Loan anatomy** — principal, interest, the rate, and the term, plus what makes a mortgage special.
- **Amortization schedules** — why early payments are almost all interest, how the split flips over time, and exactly where each month's money goes.
- **Fixed vs variable rates** — which one shifts the risk onto you, and when each makes sense.
- **APR vs APY** — how lenders actually quote rates, so you read the **total cost of credit**, not just the monthly payment.
- **Down payment, loan-to-value & mortgage insurance** — how much you put down, what ratio it leaves, and the insurance that triggers.
- **Fees & closing costs** — the charges hiding in the fine print.
- **Life after signing** — early repayment, refinancing, default, and foreclosure.

You already know interest and the APR/APY split, so this sits one rung up the ladder: point that machinery at the most common financial decision a person ever makes — **borrowing money** — and price every trade-off before you sign.
