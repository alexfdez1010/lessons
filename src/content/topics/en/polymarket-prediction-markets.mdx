Most markets price *things*; a **prediction market** prices something stranger and more useful — **the probability a future event happens**. Buy a share that pays exactly **\$1 if you're right and \$0 if you're wrong**, and the price (a penny to 99 cents) *is* the crowd's probability: pay 62¢ for "YES" and the market is telling you it's 62% sure. That one idea — **price equals probability** — turns betting on the world into a quantitative discipline you already have the tools for.

Here's the arc, from order book to oracle to edge:

- **Price equals probability** — why a \$1/\$0 payout makes the price a forecast, and why a crowd betting real money aggregates scattered information sharper than any pundit.
- **Inside Polymarket** — the largest on-chain market end to end: settlement in **USDC on Polygon**, outcome tokens in **complete \$1 sets** (one YES + one NO), **minting** a set from a dollar and **merging** it back.
- **The CLOB** — the central limit order book where YES shares trade, with its spread, depth, and slippage.
- **Resolution** — the part everyone underestimates: **UMA's optimistic oracle**, where a proposer posts an outcome with a bond, a liveness window lets anyone dispute, and disputes escalate to a token-holder vote. Get it wrong and a "winning" share pays nothing.
- **Calibration & the favorite–longshot bias** — score the market like any forecaster: do events priced at 30¢ happen 30% of the time? Longshots run rich, heavy favorites run cheap.
- **Binary arbitrage** — the cleanest free lunch in the building: a YES and a NO redeem for \$1, so any time they trade for less (or more), there's locked profit — until fees eat it.
- **Bayesian edge + Kelly sizing** — form a probability the Bayesian way, compare it to the price to measure your edge, and **size the bet with Kelly on a binary** — knowing the edge is an illusion if you're less calibrated than the crowd.
- **The real risks** — ambiguous resolution, oracle disputes, thin-book illiquidity, and the regulatory/geofencing reality.

This is an expert course: it assumes **Bayesian updating** (to form your probability) and **Kelly sizing** (to bet it) are already sharp. Bring them — every lesson cashes them out on a live, on-chain market where being right pays a dollar and being wrong pays nothing.
