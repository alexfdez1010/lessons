Ask anyone whether they'd rather have \$100 today or \$100 next year, and they'll
take it now without thinking. They're right — and the reason isn't impatience, it's
math. Money you hold today can be invested, lent, or put to work, so it quietly grows
into *more* than its face value by next year. That single idea — **money has a price
for time** — is the bedrock under interest rates, loans, bonds, valuations, mortgages,
and every DeFi yield you'll ever see.

This topic builds that bedrock from scratch. First **compounding** and **future value**
(how today's money grows forward), then **discounting** and **present value** (how
tomorrow's money shrinks back to today), and the **discount rate** that controls how
fast it shrinks. Get this straight and the rest of finance stops looking like magic.
