Anyone can show you a chart that goes up. The hard part is answering the real
questions: *How fast did it grow? How wild was the ride? Did the manager actually
add skill, or just ride the market up?* That's what investment metrics are for —
a small toolkit of numbers that turn a squiggly price chart into something you can
compare, rank, and trust.

This topic builds the toolkit from the ground up: first **return** (how much you
made), then **risk** (how much you sweated), then the **risk-adjusted** ratios that
combine the two — because a 60% gain on a calm ride is worth a lot more than the
same 60% on a near-heart-attack.
