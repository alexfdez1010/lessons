You already know what money is, how it moves, and that it earns interest over time.
This topic is the bridge to the next big idea in finance: instead of just *holding*
money or lending it at interest, you can **own things that grow** — and that's what
investing is. But the moment you step from a savings account into investing, a new word
shows up everywhere and quietly runs the whole game: **risk**.

This topic builds that bridge one plank at a time, assuming you've never bought anything
fancier than groceries. First, **what an asset actually is** — the thing you're buying
when you invest, and the two ways it can pay you. Then **saving vs investing** — why
they're not the same job, and which one to do first. Then the trade-off that governs
*every* investment decision ever made: **risk and return**, and why there's no free
lunch. Next, a plain-English tour of the three things beginners actually buy —
**stocks, bonds, and funds** — and finally, **what "a return" really means**, so a
percentage on a screen stops being mysterious. Get these five and you're ready to read
a fund factsheet without flinching.
