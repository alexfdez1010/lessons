You already know money earns interest. The next big idea: instead of just *holding*
money, you can **own things that grow** — that's investing. And the moment you step
out of a savings account, one word starts running the whole game: **risk**.

This topic builds the bridge one plank at a time, assuming you've never bought anything
fancier than groceries. You'll learn:

- **What an asset is** — the thing you're buying when you invest, and the two ways it can pay you.
- **Saving vs investing** — why they're not the same job, and which one to do first.
- **Risk and return** — the trade-off behind *every* investment decision, and why there's no free lunch.
- **Stocks, bonds, and funds** — a plain-English tour of the three things beginners actually buy.
- **What "a return" really means** — so a percentage on a screen stops being mysterious.

Get these five and you're ready to read a fund factsheet without flinching — your first real
rung up from saver to investor.
