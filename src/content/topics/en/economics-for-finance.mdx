Before you can read a market, you need to know what's pushing it around. Stock prices,
bond yields, the cost of your groceries, whether your job feels safe — all of it rides on
a handful of economic forces working in the background.

This topic teaches those forces from **absolute zero**, assuming nothing beyond knowing
what money is. No graphs you can't read, no jargon left undefined. You'll learn:

- **What economics even is** — scarcity, trade-offs, and the "opportunity cost" hiding behind every choice.
- **Supply and demand** — the see-saw that sets every price, and what makes prices jump or crash.
- **Inflation** — why a coffee costs more every year, how it's measured, and the difference between *real* and *nominal*.
- **GDP and the business cycle** — how we measure a whole economy, and why it booms and busts in waves.
- **Central banks and interest rates** — who sets the price of money, and how a rate hike ripples into your life.
- **Fiscal policy and trade** — what governments do with taxes, spending, deficits, and dealing across borders.

Get these six and the financial news stops being noise — you'll see the machinery underneath,
and every later finance course will have ground to stand on.
