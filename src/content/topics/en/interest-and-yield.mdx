Money has a clock, and *interest* is the price it charges for time. Sounds simple —
until you notice almost every rate you're quoted is misleading on purpose: a loan at
"12%" can quietly cost more, a savings account at "12%" can quietly pay more, and a
bond yielding "5%" can leave you **poorer** than when you started.

This topic untangles all three traps. You'll learn:

- **Simple vs compound interest** — earning on your principal versus earning on your interest too, and why *how often* it compounds changes the answer.
- **Compounding frequency** — why the same rate pays out differently depending on the schedule.
- **APR vs APY** — the quoted rate versus the rate you actually get once compounding is baked in.
- **Who quotes what** — why lenders and savers each advertise whichever number flatters them.
- **Nominal vs real** — stripping out inflation to see what your money can actually *buy*.

Get these three and you'll never again take a rate at face value — the foundation for every loan, savings, and bond decision that follows.
