TradFi options desks have priced and traded volatility for half a century: market makers quote a smile, vaults sell premium, and a whole risk-management apparatus hums in the background. DeFi looked at that machine and asked a wild question — **what if all of it ran on a public blockchain instead?** The answer is this course. Options now live in smart contracts; "structured products" are tokenized vaults anyone can deposit into with a click; implied volatility is piped in through oracles; and the never-expiring perpetual future broadcasts a funding rate that doubles as a real-time read on fear and positioning. The *concepts* are the same ones you met in **Volatility Trading** and **DeFi Derivatives & Perpetuals** — but the *plumbing*, the frictions, and the failure modes are genuinely new, and that is where the edge and the danger both live.

This is the expert rung where everything you know about volatility collides with everything you know about on-chain markets. The arc runs from how an option even *works* inside a smart contract to the gas-and-oracle frictions that separate a DeFi options market from a Wall Street desk:

- **On-chain options protocols** — how an option becomes a smart contract: full collateralization vs the capital-inefficiency it forces, AMM-quoted vol vs on-chain order books, European cash-settled vs physically-settled designs, and why pricing an option on-chain is so much harder than swapping two tokens.
- **DeFi option vaults (DOVs)** — the structured-product boom: covered-call and cash-secured-put vaults that auto-roll every epoch, sell options through a weekly auction, and hand depositors a headline "yield." What that yield actually is, who buys the other side, and the **DovPayoff** of selling the rally for premium.
- **The structural short-vol risk** — the catch nobody puts in the marketing: a DOV is *systematically short volatility*, harvesting the volatility risk premium with a negatively-skewed payoff. Why the auctions can underprice, why crowding makes it reflexive, and what a crash does to "yield."
- **On-chain implied vol & oracles** — where the volatility number even comes from: on-chain vol surfaces and DVOL-style indices, the **OnchainVol** problem of a laggy, sample-and-held oracle, and how a stale vol read becomes an arbitrageur's free lunch.
- **Perp funding as a vol & skew signal** — the perpetual's funding rate and basis as a live broadcast of leverage, sentiment, and the cost of carry — and how desks read funding to infer skew, positioning, and an impending volatility regime change.
- **Settlement, liquidity & frictions** — the unglamorous reality: gas costs on every roll, oracle-settled expiries and the MEV that swarms them, pin risk, fragmented liquidity, and the full TradFi-vs-DeFi comparison of what on-chain actually costs you.

By the end you will read a DeFi "yield" product and instantly see the short option underneath it, judge whether an on-chain vol quote is even trustworthy, turn a funding rate into a volatility view, and price the frictions that a glossy APY number quietly omits. A **graded final exam** runs the whole discipline back at you at the end, one locked question at a time.
