Stochastic processes handed you the rulebook for how prices wander, but a rulebook is only theory until you point it at real data. **Time-series finance is the forensics** — fit the rules to an actual return series, forecast both the next move *and* the next storm, and refuse to be fooled by a backtest that looks too good to be true.

Here's the pipeline you'll build, from raw prices to a validated, risk-aware model:

- **Stationarity & unit roots** — why a stock's **price** flunks (it carries a unit root, shocks never decay) while its **return** sails through, and how the **augmented Dickey–Fuller test**, logs, and differencing turn an un-modelable series into a modelable one.
- **Autocorrelation, ACF & PACF** — the fingerprint readers that say whether today's return predicts tomorrow's, judged against the **white-noise band** ($\pm 2/\sqrt{n}$) and confirmed with the **Ljung–Box test**.
- **AR, MA & ARIMA(p,d,q)** — the workhorse mean models (own past values, past shocks, and their combination), with order chosen honestly via AIC/BIC and validated on the residuals.
- **Volatility clustering & ARCH/GARCH** — modeling *risk itself*: the **ARCH** insight, the three-parameter **GARCH(1,1)**, and the **persistence** $\alpha + \beta$ that sets how slowly a volatility shock fades.
- **EWMA & RiskMetrics** — the one-parameter cousin behind J.P. Morgan's Value-at-Risk numbers, and why a fixed $\lambda = 0.94$ beat a fitted GARCH for firm-wide, auditable daily risk.
- **Backtesting traps** — the three deadliest ways to fool yourself (**look-ahead bias**, **overfitting**, **data-snooping**) and the discipline that defends against them: parsimony, out-of-sample holdouts, and **walk-forward analysis**.

This is the expert rung where the abstract machinery of randomness-through-time becomes a working pipeline you can run on tonight's closing prices — and a **graded final exam** runs the whole thing back at you, one locked question at a time.
